Eq Art Exhibition

United Kingdom

In today's age, connection is rarely made.

This results in a waste of money and resources for organizations looking to advertise. Further, the cost read more to advertise on social media has increased 2-4X in the last ten years. As a result, brands are left with an uncomfortable feeling when social media advertising does not go as planned. The common advertising strategy is to be louder: use flashy design and pay more to be seen more. With the Eq exhibition being so unique, a different approach was necessary.

Through collaboration with the exhibition curator, a 4-part video series was created: SOMVO, Story of My Very Own. The Eq exhibition poses deep questions regarding moral responsibility and being active in one's spiritual practice. Based on the exhibition’s intent, the video series discusses internal change and transformation. The video series takes us through the first-hand experiences of the main character who, with the help of his friends, is trying to navigate a tough situation. The viewer creates unique associations to the episodes based on their own personal journey.

Episode 1: “You’ll Only Know if You Go Forward”
Raj, Mannys friend, reaches out to talk but finds the voicemail on the other side so he explains his thinking. Visually, vast landscapes contrasted with small-scale objects depict a state of loneliness. The sound design provides an element of contemplation.

Episode 2: “What is Your Pain?”
Mannys mental instability is revealed. Visuals of nature provide a space for self-reflection. The sound design creates a space for Manny to open up about what's on his mind.

Episode 3: “From the Ground Up”
Mannys younger cousin urges him to keep at it. Visuals of buildings and concrete foster the act of building oneself back up while the sound design adds an element of reassurance.

Episode 4: “ A Friend Who Watches Over”
Raj and another friend experience something powerful. They reflect on Mannys state, foreshadowing a positive space to come to. Visuals demonstrate introspection and the sound design provides a new chance.

Instead of paying to advertise, content reaches people organically through shares, comments, and tags. As videos release, the puzzle is pieced together. People are provoked to reflect internally about the videos, which embody the exhibitions messaging.

In today's social media landscape, leaving things up to interpretation creates an environment where people don't feel sold to. People are active in viewing, which generates a community of participants.
